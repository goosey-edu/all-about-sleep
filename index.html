The 4 Stages of Sleep

Sleep is divided into REM sleep and 3 types of Non-REM (NREM) sleep

Stage 1: Lightest Sleep

Your heart rate, eye movements, and brain waves slow down.

This stage only happens when you are first falling asleep.

Stage 2: Light Sleep

Your body continues to relax. During this stage you can be woken up easier.

Your body temperature decreases and your eye movements stop.

Stage 3: Lightest Sleep

This is the stage where your body and energy restores and replenishes itself.

Your tissues and muscles repair itself.

Stage 4: Rapid Eye Movement (REM) Sleep

This stage is where your mind restores itself. Your brain process informations and preforms functions that aid in memory and learning.

This stage is also when dreaming occurs.
